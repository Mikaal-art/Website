It has become a major part of people's daily lives. It is hard to imagine the Internet without dynamic graphics, different typography styles, background, videos and music.

introduction to web and web design

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee, while working at CERN, proposed the creation of a global hypertext project, which later became known as the World Wide Web.1991: The Web Emerges, Bridging the World. Text-only pages can be viewed using a simple line mode browser. [2] In 1993 Marc Andreessen and Eric Bina created the Mosaic browser.

The evolution of web design

In 1996, Microsoft released its first competing browser, complete with its own features and HTML tags. It was also the first browser to support style sheets, which were seen as an obscure authoring technique at the time and are an important aspect of web design today. HTML markup for tables was originally intended to display tabular data.Early Web Pioneers: Hacking Tables for Layout Innovation. At the time, as design and good aesthetics seemed to take precedence over good markup structure, and little attention was paid to semantics and web accessibility.

Tools and technologies

Technologies used to create websites include W3C standards such as HTML and CSS, which can be hand-coded or created using WYSIWYG editing software. Marketing and communication design

Marketing and communication design on a website can indicate what works for its target market. It can be an age group or a particular part of a culture. In this way the designer can understand the tendencies of his audience. Designers may also consider the type of website they are designing, meaning, for example, whether the design considerations of a (B2B) business-to-business website are the same as those of a consumer-targeted website such as retail. or may be very different from an entertainment website. Careful consideration may be given to ensure that the aesthetics or overall design of a site does not conflict with the clarity and accuracy of content or ease of web navigation, [10] particularly on a B2B website. Designers may also consider the reputation of the owner or business to ensure the site is representative.

Page layout

A well-designed page layout is the foundation for a strong user interface. To maximize user engagement, ensure your layout is unique and user-friendly. For example, a designer may consider that a site's page layout should remain the same across different pages when designing the layout. Page pixel width can also be considered important for aligning objects in layout design. The most popular fixed-width websites usually have a single width set to match the current most popular browser window, at the current most popular screen resolution, at the current most popular monitor size. Most pages are also centered for aesthetics concerns on larger screens.

Motion graphics

The use of motion graphics can also affect the page layout and user interface. The choice of whether or not to use motion graphics may depend on the website's target market. Motion graphics can be expected or at least received better than an entertainment website. However, a website target audience with a more serious or formal interest (such as business, community, or government) may find animations unnecessary and annoying if only for entertainment or decoration purposes. This doesn't mean that more serious content can't be enhanced with animated or video presentations that relate to the content. In either case, motion graphic design can make the difference between a more effective visual or a distracting visual. Motion graphics that are not initiated by the site visitor may cause accessibility issues. The World Wide Web Consortium's accessibility standards require that site visitors can disable animations.
